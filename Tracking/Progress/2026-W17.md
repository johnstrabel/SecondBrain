## Connections Made This Week

### Non-obvious: Trade Creation/Diversion ↔ Informed/Noise Trading
[[Sapir (2011)]] distinguishes trade creation (efficient reallocation) from trade diversion (inefficient substitution). In [[Reddit as Financial Signal]], the same split appears: some Reddit attention reflects genuine information accumulation (informed → trade creation equivalent), while velocity spikes preceding negative returns (H2 result: −32 bps) represent noise-driven diversion — attention pulling capital away from fundamental value. Both frameworks describe conditions under which integration/aggregation of signals improves or degrades outcomes.

### Non-obvious: Agglomeration Effects ↔ Meme Stock Attention Concentration
The lecture's core-periphery dynamic location effect (economic activity concentrating in rich cores, leaving periphery behind) is structurally identical to Reddit mention concentration: GME alone has 161,489 mentions out of 2.7M total — 5.9% of the entire dataset. The same agglomeration logic applies: network effects, liquidity, and visibility reinforce concentration. [[Sentiment Velocity]] spikes for meme stocks (GME, AMC) are essentially the financial equivalent of agglomeration shocks.

### Non-obvious: EMU Design Flaw ↔ H4 Counter-Directional Result
Balassa's warning: monetary union without fiscal stabilisers produces instability when asymmetric shocks hit. [[Post-Earnings Announcement Drift]] with high [[Sentiment Velocity]]: the H4 finding shows that high Reddit attention *suppresses* PEAD at 20 days (Q4 surprise + Q4 velocity = +0.199% vs Q4 surprise + Q1 velocity = +0.671%). Both results say the same thing: adding an unanchored second signal without a corrective mechanism (fiscal union / fundamental anchor) produces worse outcomes than the single signal alone.

The lecture explicitly discussed the EU Capital Markets Union as the next frontier — startups going to the US for capital because EU markets are fragmented. Mapletree operates at this exact intersection: Singapore-based, pan-Asian capital deployment, REIT structure as cross-border capital vehicle.

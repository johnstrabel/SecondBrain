# Learning Log — 2026-04-20

## New Knowledge from Meetings

### REIT Structure as a Tax Vehicle (from [[2026-04-20 Phone call with Dad]])

- **REITs as capital gains shelter:** When selling real estate, taking REIT shares instead of cash defers taxable event; shares held until long-term capital gains threshold (lower tax rate than short-term)
- **First Industrial REIT:** $9–10B market cap, stock ~$64; self-funded (no bank debt), self-performing operations; originated by Scheidler Group rolling up regional real estate companies into a single REIT vehicle
- **REIT structure vs. regular real estate:** REITs distribute ≥90% of taxable income as dividends (IRS requirement), trade on exchanges like equities, provide liquidity that direct real estate ownership doesn't

### Mapletree Capital Group (Singapore)

- **AUM:** $80.3B under management, $637M recurring capital
- **Structure:** Parent holding company + three Singapore-listed REITs + investment company with loan services
- **Global footprint:** One of the largest Asia-Pacific real estate managers; comparable in structure to large US REIT operators
- **Relevance:** Potential employer; operates at the intersection of real estate, capital markets, and asset management — connects to thesis themes (fundamental valuation, asset pricing)

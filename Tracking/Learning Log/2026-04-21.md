### EU Integration Theory — Welfare Effects of Integration (from [[Sources/Lectures/Econ/Raw/Transcript April 21st Pt.1]])

**Three types of welfare effects (Sapir/Balassa):**

- **Static effects:** Trade creation (efficient reallocation toward comparative advantage — *gain*) vs. trade diversion (common external tariff redirects to more expensive partner — *loss*). One-time level shift; net effect depends on which dominates.
- **Dynamic effects — the three mechanisms:**
  1. *Economies of scale* — larger market enables minimum efficient scale; lower average costs
  2. *Competition* — exposure to EU rivals forces down prices, reduces X-inefficiency
  3. *Investment/FDI* — integration attracts capital into the bloc; productivity growth
- **Location effects:**
  - *Static:* Heckscher-Ohlin factor endowments → labour-intensive production shifts east, capital-intensive stays west
  - *Dynamic:* Agglomeration (Krugman NEG) → core-periphery risk; activity concentrates in rich cores; EU Structural Funds are the corrective
- **Short vs long run:** Short run dominated by adjustment costs and trade diversion; long run dominated by compounding dynamic effects (5–10% GDP gain empirically attributed to EU integration)

**EMU design flaw:** Monetary union without fiscal union → asymmetric shock problem → Eurozone crisis 2010 was foreseeable; Balassa predicted it in 1961.

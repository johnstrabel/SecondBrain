# Green (2010) — Has Europe Solved the Problem of War?

**Full citation:** Green, P. (2010). Has Europe Solved the Problem of War? *European Review*, 18(3), 343–356.

## Summary

Examines whether European integration has succeeded in eliminating war as a means of resolving conflicts between EU member states. Argues that while interstate war within the EU is effectively inconceivable, the EU has not "solved" war in the broader sense — it has exported security externally through NATO and EU enlargement, but regional conflicts outside EU borders persist, and EU security capacity remains limited.

## Key Arguments

1. **Internal peace achieved:** No war between EU member states since founding — a remarkable historical achievement.
2. **Kantian peace:** Democratic peace theory supports the claim that liberal democracies bound by trade do not fight each other.
3. **Conditionality as peace mechanism:** EU enlargement requires democratic governance and rule of law — extends the peace zone.
4. **External limitations:** EU failed to prevent or quickly resolve the Yugoslav wars; required NATO intervention.
5. **Defense integration gap:** EU lacks a credible independent military capability; relies on NATO (US leadership) for hard security.

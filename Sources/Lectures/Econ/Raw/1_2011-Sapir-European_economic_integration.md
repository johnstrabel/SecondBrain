# Sapir (2011) — European Integration at the Crossroads

**Full citation:** Sapir, A. (2011). European Integration at the Crossroads: Review of Balassa's "The Theory of Economic Integration" (Routledge, 2011 reprint). *Journal of Economic Literature*, 49(4), 1200–1229.

## Summary

Review essay occasioned by the reprint of Bela Balassa's 1961 classic *The Theory of Economic Integration*. Traces the development of European economic integration theory and practice from the Treaty of Rome (1957) to the post-Maastricht era. Evaluates what Balassa got right and wrong, and where integration theory stands today given the Eurozone debt crisis context of 2011.

## Key Arguments

1. **Balassa's framework:** Five stages of integration: Free Trade Area → Customs Union → Common Market → Economic Union → Political Union.
2. **Static vs. dynamic gains:** Trade creation vs. trade diversion (static); economies of scale, competition, investment flows (dynamic).
3. **EMU design flaws:** Balassa anticipated that monetary union without fiscal union would be unstable — prescient given the 2010 Eurozone crisis.
4. **Factor mobility:** Common market requires free movement of goods, services, capital, and labor — the "four freedoms."
5. **Political economy:** Integration is both an economic and political project; national sovereignty constraints limit deeper integration.

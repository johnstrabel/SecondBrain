# Hong & Wu (2014) — Can Momentum Factors be used to Enhance Accounting Information based Fundamental Analysis in Explaining Stock Price Movements?

**Full citation:** Hong, K.J. & Wu, E. (2014). Can Momentum Factors be used to Enhance Accounting Information based Fundamental Analysis in Explaining Stock Price Movements? QFRC Research Paper 346, University of Technology Sydney.

## Summary

Investigates whether price momentum can enhance the explanatory power of fundamental accounting variables for cross-sectional stock returns. Uses OLS and state-space modeling on Russell 3000 constituents (1999–2012). Finds: (1) both momentum and accounting variables matter; (2) accounting variables dominate at longer horizons; (3) momentum dominates at shorter horizons; (4) firm size moderates the relationship.

## Key Arguments

1. **Complementarity:** Momentum and accounting variables together explain more of SPM than either alone — supports information fusion.
2. **Horizon dependence:** Fundamental analysis is better long-term; momentum is better short-term — different signals have different optimal horizons.
3. **State-space model:** Latent common factor controls for omitted variable bias; produces cleaner estimates.
4. **Size effect:** Accounting information is more useful for small and mid-cap stocks; momentum is important across the board.
5. **Weak-form EMH:** Results are consistent with weak-form efficiency (momentum) while challenging semi-strong efficiency (accounting signals).

## Relevance to Thesis

- Provides the "combinations beat components" evidence that is the intellectual foundation of the thesis
- Horizon dependence directly relevant: velocity ratio (short-term attention) vs. PEAD (longer-term drift)
- The thesis's H4 tests a version of this interaction: does velocity amplify PEAD?

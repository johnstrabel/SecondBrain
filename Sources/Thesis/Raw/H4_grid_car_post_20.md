## What This File Contains

4×4 grid of mean CAR[+1,+20] for each surprise × velocity quartile cell.

## Key Finding

At the 20-day horizon, PEAD is clearly visible in raw means: Q4 positive surprise averages +0.0046 (4.6 bps mean), while Q1 negative surprise averages −0.0026. However, the velocity amplification hypothesis is contradicted: Q4 positive surprise with lowest velocity (Q1) earns +67 bps, but with highest velocity (Q4) earns only +20 bps — the **opposite** of H4's prediction. High velocity appears to suppress rather than amplify PEAD.

## Interpretation

The reversal-of-sign in the velocity interaction may reflect: (1) high-velocity positive surprises are already priced in during the pre-event window, (2) noise-trading at announcement absorbs some of the drift, or (3) measurement noise at the quartile level.

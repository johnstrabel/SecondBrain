# Jensen, Kelly & Pedersen (2023) — Is There a Replication Crisis in Finance?

**Full citation:** Jensen, T.I., Kelly, B. & Pedersen, L.H. (2023). Is There a Replication Crisis in Finance? *Journal of Finance*, 78(5), 2465–2518.

## Summary

Challenges claims that most finance factors cannot be replicated. Develops a Bayesian factor-replication model and finds: (1) majority of factors can be replicated; (2) factors cluster into 13 themes, most significant in tangency portfolio; (3) factors hold out-of-sample in 93 countries; (4) large number of observed factors *strengthens* rather than weakens evidence for each factor.

## Key Arguments

1. **Against the replication crisis narrative:** Hou, Xue & Zhang (2020) found 35% replication rate; Jensen et al. find 55.6% baseline, and even higher when using alpha (not raw returns) and appropriate factor construction.
2. **Bayesian pooling:** Evidence for one factor strengthens priors for related factors; the large factor zoo is evidence, not noise.
3. **13 thematic clusters:** Low risk, momentum, value, quality, etc. — each cluster is a valid risk/anomaly category.
4. **International replication:** Out-of-sample in 93 countries confirms genuine risk premia, not US data mining.
5. **Alpha vs. raw return:** Raw return can mislead — use alpha; a low-beta factor may have insignificant raw return but significant alpha.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Relevant to robustness framing for the thesis's null results (H1, H3, H4)
- ML AUC-ROC of 0.520 in thesis fits the broader pattern: most individual signals have modest alpha
- Supports the thesis's information fusion approach: combining many weak signals (Bayesian pooling logic)

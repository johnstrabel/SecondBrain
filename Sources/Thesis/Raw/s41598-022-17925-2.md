# Mancini et al. (2022) — Self-Induced Consensus of Reddit Users to Characterise the GameStop Short Squeeze

**Full citation:** Mancini, A., Desiderio, A., Di Clemente, R. & Cimini, G. (2022). Self-induced consensus of Reddit users to characterise the GameStop short squeeze. *Scientific Reports*, 12, 13780.

## Summary

Characterizes the temporal structure and sentiment evolution of WSB conversations leading up to the Jan 2021 GME short squeeze. Shows that GME-related comment occurrence and sentiment increased significantly *before* the squeeze. Introduces an opinion dynamics model where user engagement triggers a self-reinforcing consensus mechanism — collective coordination on Reddit as an early warning signal.

## Key Arguments

1. **Early warning:** Z-scores for GME ticker occurrence peaked at specific events months before the squeeze (earnings, RC Ventures stake, Microsoft partnership announcement).
2. **Opinion dynamics model:** User engagement → self-reinforcing consensus → collective action (short squeeze coordination).
3. **Ticker distribution:** All tickers follow a power law; GME is an extreme outlier as the conversation was monopolized.
4. **Conversation trees:** Users with high comment frequency also attracted more replies — hierarchical amplification structure.
5. **Sentiment as signal:** Sentiment change (not just volume) was an early indicator distinct from simple mention count.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Provides network/opinion dynamics perspective on the attention/velocity mechanism in the thesis
- The self-reinforcing consensus model explains *why* velocity spikes occur (coordinated attention, not just random noise)
- Early warning signal timing parallels the thesis's pre-event sentiment features (car_pre_5 window)

# Easton (2007) — Estimating the Cost of Capital Implied by Market Prices and Accounting Data

**Full citation:** Easton, P. (2007). Estimating the Cost of Capital Implied by Market Prices and Accounting Data. *Foundations and Trends in Accounting*, 2(4), 241–364.

## Summary

Comprehensive survey of methods for estimating the expected rate of return (cost of capital) by inverting accounting-based valuation models against market prices. Covers discounted cash flow models, residual income valuation (Ohlson 1995), abnormal growth in earnings model (Ohlson-Juettner-Nauroth 2005), and their empirical implementations. Discusses pros and cons of each approach relative to CAPM/factor-model-based estimates.

## Key Arguments

1. **Model inversion:** ICC = find r that makes PV of forecasted earnings/dividends equal to observed market price.
2. **Ohlson (1995) RIM:** Re-expresses dividend discount model in terms of earnings and book value — requires only analyst EPS forecasts and book values.
3. **Ohlson-JN AGM:** Focuses on earnings growth — links cost of capital to Price-to-Earnings ratio.
4. **Advantage over factor models:** Fama-French (1997) call realized return estimates "unavoidably imprecise"; ICC avoids noise in historical returns.
5. **Survey contribution:** Reviews the burgeoning empirical literature using ICC for governance, disclosure, accounting standards research.

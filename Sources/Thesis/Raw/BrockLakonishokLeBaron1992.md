# Brock, Lakonishok & LeBaron (1992) — Simple Technical Trading Rules and the Stochastic Properties of Stock Returns

**Full citation:** Brock, W., Lakonishok, J. & LeBaron, B. (1992). Simple Technical Trading Rules and the Stochastic Properties of Stock Returns. *Journal of Finance*, 47(5), 1731–1764.

## Summary

*Note: PDF text extraction failed (corrupt xref table). Description based on well-known paper content.*

Studies 26 simple technical trading rules (moving average crossovers and trading range break rules) applied to the DJIA from 1897 to 1986. Finds that buy signals generate higher returns and lower variance than sell signals — technical trading rules have predictive power. Widely cited as the benchmark study that established the profitability of technical analysis before the data-snooping critique.

## Key Arguments

1. **Buy vs. sell signals:** MA buy signals generate mean daily returns of 0.042%; sell signals generate −0.025%; difference of 0.067%/day is economically large.
2. **Lower variance for buy signals:** Counter to predictions of standard asset pricing models.
3. **Bootstrap tests:** Results survive bootstrap simulations; not explained by simple time-series models (GARCH, EGARCH, ARIMA).
4. **26 rules tested:** Variable-length MA crossovers and fixed-length "trading range break" rules; all generate positive results.
5. **Benchmark:** Became the reference point for subsequent data-snooping critique (Sullivan, Timmermann & White 1999).

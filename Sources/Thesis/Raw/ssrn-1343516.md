# Hou, van Dijk & Zhang (2009) — The Implied Cost of Capital: A New Approach

**Full citation:** Hou, K., van Dijk, M.A. & Zhang, Y. (2012). The Implied Cost of Capital: A New Approach. *Journal of Accounting and Economics*, 53(3), 504–526. (SSRN 2009)

## Summary

Proposes an ICC approach that does not rely on analyst earnings forecasts (which are biased and have limited coverage). Instead, uses a cross-sectional model to forecast firm earnings. This approach allows ICC estimation for a much larger sample (including small firms, longer time series, no analyst coverage required). The cross-sectional earnings model outperforms consensus analyst forecasts.

## Key Arguments

1. **Problem with analyst forecasts:** Biased (optimistic), limited coverage (IBES starts 1980, tilts toward large firms), missing long-horizon forecasts.
2. **Cross-sectional earnings model:** Regress future earnings on current profitability, B/M, size, lagged returns — predicts earnings for all firms.
3. **ICC without analysts:** Broader sample, longer history, more reliable proxy for expected returns.
4. **Equity premium implications:** Cross-sectional ICC aggregated to market level provides cleaner equity premium estimates.
5. **Anomaly tests:** ICC-based tests of asset pricing anomalies produce more reliable inference than realized-return tests.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Extends the fundamental valuation literature (Gebhardt et al., Easton) with a data-expansion approach
- Motivates the thesis's use of cross-sectional features rather than analyst forecast data in the ML model

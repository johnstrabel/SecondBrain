# Betzer & Harries (2022) — How Online Discussion Board Activity Affects Stock Trading: The Case of GameStop

**Full citation:** Betzer, A. & Harries, J.P. (2022). How online discussion board activity affects stock trading: the case of GameStop. *Financial Markets and Portfolio Management*, 36, 443–472.

## Summary

Studies the relationship between Reddit WallStreetBets posts and GameStop trading activity during the Jan 2021 short squeeze (>40M WSB posts, Jan 2020 – Mar 2021). Finds significant positive correlation between Reddit post count and trading volume/volatility in the following 30-minute window (attention mechanism). No evidence that Reddit posts are informative for future price changes.

## Key Arguments

1. **Attention mechanism:** Post count predicts next 30-minute trading volume and volatility — confirms attention-based (not informational) mechanism.
2. **Option market extension:** Also applies to option volume — novel contribution; retail option trading is a recent phenomenon.
3. **Retail vs. institutional:** Disentangles effect on retail vs. non-retail trading; Reddit posts primarily affect retail flow.
4. **No informativeness:** Post content does not predict future price changes — consistent with noise trading rather than information transmission.
5. **Scale:** GameStop rose 20× in January 2021 without discernible fundamental news; ~4M+ posts attributed to GME alone.

## Key Quotes

> "While we find a significant and positive relationship between Reddit posts and various trading measures in the following 30-min window in accordance with an attention-based mechanism, our results offer no indication for the informativeness of Reddit posts."

## Relevance to Thesis

- Strong precedent for the attention-based (not informational) interpretation of Reddit activity
- Consistent with the thesis's H1 null (sentiment p=0.72) and H2's noise-trading reversion finding
- Attention mechanism parallels the thesis's velocity signal (spikes in mention count)
- Provides justification for including Google Trends and Wikipedia page views as attention signals

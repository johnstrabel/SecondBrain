# Huang, Wang & Yang (2022) — FinBERT: A Large Language Model for Extracting Information from Financial Text

**Full citation:** Huang, A.H., Wang, H. & Yang, Y. (2023). FinBERT: A Large Language Model for Extracting Information from Financial Text. *Contemporary Accounting Research*, 40(2), 806–841. (First version 2019; SSRN 2022)

## Summary

Develops FinBERT, a domain-adapted BERT model pre-trained on large financial text corpora and fine-tuned for financial sentiment classification. Benchmarked against Loughran-McDonald dictionary, Naive Bayes, SVM, random forest, CNN, and LSTM — FinBERT substantially outperforms all. Advantage is especially pronounced for sentences other algorithms misclassify as neutral.

## Key Arguments

1. **Domain adaptation:** General BERT pre-trained on Wikipedia/BookCorpus; FinBERT additionally pre-trained on financial filings, analyst reports, earnings call transcripts (4.9B tokens).
2. **Contextual understanding:** Unlike bag-of-words methods, FinBERT captures word-order and context — critical for financial jargon (e.g., "liability" is negative, "revenue" is neutral in general text but context-dependent in financial text).
3. **Benchmark superiority:** FinBERT outperforms all tested algorithms across F1, precision, recall on 10,000 labelled analyst report sentences.
4. **Neutral misclassification:** Other algorithms fail on nuanced positive/negative sentences; FinBERT's contextual encoding prevents this.
5. **LLM tradeoffs:** Acknowledges that LLMs require substantial compute and are opaque ("black boxes").

## Relevance to Thesis

- This is the FinBERT paper that justifies use of ProsusAI/finbert (a related variant) for scoring 2.74M Reddit mentions
- Domain mismatch caveat: Huang et al.'s FinBERT trained on formal financial text; applying to Reddit slang degrades performance — noted in thesis as a limitation
- The 83.6% neutral rate in the Reddit corpus likely reflects this domain mismatch

# Cochrane (2011) — Presidential Address: Discount Rates

**Full citation:** Cochrane, J.H. (2011). Discount Rates. *Journal of Finance*, 66(4), 1047–1108. (AFA Presidential Address)

## Summary

Argues that discount-rate variation is the central organizing question of modern asset pricing. Documents that all price-dividend ratio variation corresponds to discount-rate variation (not cash-flow variation), upending the earlier view that prices reflect expected earnings. Surveys the "zoo" of new cross-sectional factors and calls for theoretical consolidation. Applications span portfolio theory, cost of capital, accounting, and macroeconomics.

## Key Arguments

1. **Return predictability:** D/P ratio predicts long-horizon returns; 5-year R² = 0.28; one pp increase in D/Y forecasts ~4 pp higher return.
2. **No cash-flow predictability:** Dividend growth is essentially unpredictable; all D/P variation is discount-rate variation.
3. **Factor zoo:** Hundreds of cross-sectional return predictors have been discovered; they need theoretical consolidation, not proliferation.
4. **Discount rates vs. mispricing:** Most apparent anomalies likely reflect risk premia or discount-rate movements, not simple mispricing.
5. **Unified framework:** Discount-rate variation links time-series predictability, cross-section, portfolio theory, and corporate finance.

## Key Quotes

> "We have a zoo of new factors." [Motivates Jensen et al. 2023 replication crisis paper]

## Relevance to Thesis

- Provides theoretical backdrop for why simple sentiment signals may not predict returns (H1 null)
- Discount-rate variation context: short-run sentiment velocity effects may be discount-rate noise, not fundamental news
- The "factor zoo" framing motivates the thesis's focus on signal combination (information fusion) rather than any single factor

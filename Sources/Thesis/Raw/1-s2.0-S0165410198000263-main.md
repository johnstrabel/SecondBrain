# Frankel & Lee (1998) — Accounting Valuation, Market Expectation, and Cross-Sectional Stock Returns

**Full citation:** Frankel, R. & Lee, C.M.C. (1998). Accounting valuation, market expectation, and cross-sectional stock returns. *Journal of Accounting and Economics*, 25(3), 283–319.

## Summary

Uses I/B/E/S analyst consensus forecasts and the residual income (Edwards-Bell-Ohlson) model to estimate fundamental firm value V. Shows that the V/P ratio is a strong predictor of cross-sectional long-horizon returns, outperforming the B/P ratio over 2–3 year windows. Strategy can be improved by incorporating analyst forecast errors.

## Key Arguments

1. **Residual income model (EBO):** V* = Book value + PV of expected excess returns (ROE − cost of capital); requires only book value and earnings forecasts.
2. **V*/P ratio:** Highly correlated with contemporaneous stock prices (explains >70% of cross-sectional variation in recent years); predicts long-term returns beyond B/P.
3. **Market efficiency implication:** V*/P > 1 predicts future outperformance — analysts' earnings forecasts impound information not fully reflected in prices.
4. **Returns:** At 12-month horizon, V*/P performs similarly to B/P; at 2–3 years, V*/P dominates.
5. **Forecast errors:** Incorporating analyst forecast revision errors further improves the strategy.

## Key Quotes

> "The V*/P ratio is a good predictor of long-term cross-sectional returns. This effect is not due to standard risk proxies."

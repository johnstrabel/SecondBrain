# Kirtac & Germano (2024) — Sentiment Trading with Large Language Models

**Full citation:** Kirtac, K. & Germano, G. (2024). Sentiment trading with large language models. *Finance Research Letters*, 62, 105227.

## Summary

Benchmarks LLMs (OPT, BERT, FinBERT) alongside the Loughran-McDonald dictionary for sentiment analysis of 965,375 US financial news articles (2010–2023). GPT-based approaches outperform all others in trading strategy performance. FinBERT shows domain advantage over general BERT but is surpassed by GPT. All models compared in trading simulation incorporating transaction costs.

## Key Arguments

1. **GPT superiority:** GPT-based models outperform FinBERT, BERT, and Loughran-McDonald in return prediction from news sentiment.
2. **Domain specialization:** FinBERT outperforms general BERT for financial text — confirms domain pre-training value.
3. **Probing technique:** Uses feature extraction rather than fine-tuning; "white box" in that sentiment weights are interpretable.
4. **Trading simulation:** Strategy performance evaluated with realistic transaction costs and news timing alignment.
5. **Sentiment dynamics:** LLMs capture nuanced, context-dependent sentiment that dictionaries and bag-of-words methods miss.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Shows that FinBERT (used in thesis) is not the state-of-the-art — GPT models outperform it
- Reinforces that the 5.9% feature importance for avg_sentiment_score in the thesis's ML model reflects FinBERT's limitations
- Future work direction: replacing ProsusAI/finbert with GPT-4 sentiment scoring on Reddit data

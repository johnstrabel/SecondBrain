# Gebhardt, Lee & Swaminathan (2001) — Toward an Implied Cost of Capital

**Full citation:** Gebhardt, W.R., Lee, C.M.C. & Swaminathan, B. (2001). Toward an Implied Cost of Capital. *Journal of Accounting Research*, 39(1), 135–176.

## Summary

Proposes estimating the cost of equity capital by inverting the discounted residual income model against observed market prices. Identifies firm characteristics systematically related to the implied cost of capital (ICC): industry membership, B/M ratio, forecasted long-term growth, and analyst forecast dispersion. Together, these explain ~60% of cross-sectional ICC variation.

## Key Arguments

1. **ICC via RIM inversion:** Back out expected return r such that PV of residual incomes = current market price.
2. **Predictors of ICC:** B/M, industry, long-term growth forecasts, analyst forecast dispersion — stable cross-sectional relationships exploitable for cost-of-capital estimation.
3. **Advantage over realized returns:** ICC is forward-looking (ex ante), not subject to noise in realized return series.
4. **Stability:** Long-term cross-sectional relations are stable enough to use for forecasting future costs of capital.
5. **Applications:** Capital budgeting, investment decisions, valuation research — particularly relevant for discounted residual income models.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Directly related to the fundamental valuation pillar of the thesis's hierarchical framework
- Residual income model is the same framework as Frankel & Lee (1998) — extended to cost-of-capital estimation
- Cited in Chapter 2 as part of the accounting-based valuation literature

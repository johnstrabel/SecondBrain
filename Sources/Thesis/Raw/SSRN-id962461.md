# Faber (2006/2013) — A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation

**Full citation:** Faber, M.T. (2007). A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation. *Journal of Wealth Management*, Spring 2007. Updated 2009, 2013.

## Summary

Presents a simple quantitative tactical asset allocation (TAA) system using 10-month simple moving average as the only timing signal. When price > 10-month SMA, hold; below, move to cash/T-bills. Applied to five asset classes (US stocks, foreign stocks, bonds, REITs, commodities) with equal weighting and annual rebalancing. Achieves equity-like returns with bond-like volatility and drawdowns. Validated out-of-sample through the 2008–2012 update.

## Key Arguments

1. **Simple moving average timing:** 10-month SMA is the sole signal; avoids complex optimization.
2. **5 asset classes:** US stocks (S&P 500), MSCI EAFE, Barclays Aggregate Bond, GSCI Commodities, NAREIT — equally weighted.
3. **Performance:** Equity-like returns (~10%/year) with ~7% volatility and <25% max drawdown vs. S&P 500's 50%+ drawdown in 2008–09.
4. **2008 validation:** Strategy avoided the worst of the 2008 crisis by moving to cash as markets fell below 10-month SMA.
5. **Extensions:** Adding more asset classes, leverage, alternative cash strategies — all explored in 2013 update.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Practitioner evidence that simple price-based rules (moving averages) have risk management value
- Supports the technical analysis pillar; MAs are a simple form of momentum/trend following
- The 10-month MA is a close analog to the 90-day baseline window used in the thesis's velocity ratio

# Warkulat & Pelster (2024) — Social Media Attention and Retail Investor Behavior: Evidence from r/wallstreetbets

**Full citation:** Warkulat, S. & Pelster, M. (2024). Social media attention and retail investor behavior: Evidence from r/wallstreetbets. *International Review of Financial Analysis*, 96, 103574.

## Summary

Uses individual trading data from a large online brokerage to show that WSB attention (1) spurs uninformed trading including short positions, (2) increases overall portfolio risk (riskier stocks, larger position sizes), and (3) significantly reduces holding period returns (HPR). Positions created at peak WSB attention realize −8.5% HPR vs. positive average.

## Key Arguments

1. **Individual-level data:** Unlike prior studies using proxies, directly observes individual investor trades and returns.
2. **Risk-taking increase:** WSB attention causes investors to trade riskier stocks and allocate larger portfolio shares — consistent with FOMO and investor incompetence.
3. **Harmful welfare effects:** −8.5% HPR for high-attention trades; average HPR across all investments is positive — WSB attention destroys value.
4. **Short positions:** WSB attention also increases uninformed short-selling — irrational in both directions.
5. **Information asymmetry dissolution:** Internet has reduced information asymmetry (Drake et al., 2017), enabling retail participation but not necessarily rational participation.

## Relevance to Thesis

- Confirms that Reddit/WSB attention is primarily noise trading rather than informed trading
- Welfare degradation aligns with H2's −32 bps result: velocity spikes → noise-trading reversion
- Cross-sectional perspective: retail investors are most affected — supports H3's premise (even if results are null)

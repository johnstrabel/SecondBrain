# Final Interview — Bankers Life (Greg Palutke, Lauren Loveless)

## Summary

Final interview with Bankers Life. Greg and Lauren walked through the full employment structure, compensation model, licensing requirements, and career progression. Role is 1099 contractor (not W2). Start date August 1st. Follow-up scheduled May 15th.

## Key Points

### Employment Structure
- **1099 contractor** — not W2; self-manage quarterly/annual taxes
- Set aside ~20% of income for taxes
- All work expenses tax-deductible (mileage, computer, etc.)
- **Health insurance:** reimbursement after 6 months (must show proof of coverage; company uses UnitedHealthcare)
- **Leadership roles** (~1 year in): 401k equivalent, stock purchase options, traditional health benefits

### Compensation & Commission
- Average APC (Annual Paid Commission) per policy: **~$600**
- Commission rates by product:
  - Life insurance: **35–65%** of annual premium
  - Medicare: **20% first year + 20% residual** (industry standard is only 5–7% residual)
- **Residual income** continues after 8 years even if you leave — example: former employee still earns $80k+/year
- Weekly pay; timing varies by policy type (some immediate, some require medical records first)
- 50/50 split when working with partners/mentors

### Lead Generation
- ~25 leads distributed weekly across ~40 agents
- Individual allocation varies by team availability
- **48-hour contact window** before leads go to competitors
- Lead conversion rate: ~5% (industry average 2.9%)
- Average lead cost: $17 — company covers
- Referral leads outperform cold leads

### Licensing & Career Progression
- State-specific insurance licenses required; residence license + non-resident licenses ($100/year per state); no re-testing for additional states
- Financial advisor license is federal (FINRA/SEC regulated)
- Attrition pattern: most leave during exam/study phase; high retention after first few months; some leave at ~3 years to start own agencies
- **Start date: August 1st** (corrected from July 1st)

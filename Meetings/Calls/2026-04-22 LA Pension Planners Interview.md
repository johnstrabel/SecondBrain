# Interview — LA Pension Planners (Financial Advisor Role)

## Summary

Interview for financial advisor role at LA Pension Planners. 1099 contractor position focused on public school employee clients. High volume cold calling early career. Four licenses required before starting. 90% industry turnover rate flagged. Next step: Friday info session at 2pm Pacific.

## Key Points

### Position Structure
- **1099 contractor** — clients belong to advisor, not the company
- Remote work with monthly in-person meetings at Pasadena office
- ~40 advisors at firm
- Two compensation options:
  1. $30k salary + 50% commissions/fees (standard for new advisors)
  2. Full commissions/fees, no salary (after gaining experience)

### Lead Generation & Territory
- Assigned territories: school districts nationwide + private companies
- Primary focus: **public school employees**
- Methods: cold calling, email outreach — high volume expected early on
- **90% industry turnover rate** (key risk factor)

### Licensing Required Before Starting
1. Series 66
2. Series 7
3. California Life & Health
4. SIE (Securities Industry Essentials)

### Training & Onboarding
- Daily training: economy, investments, client meetings
- Mentor-led meetings for first 6 months
- Goal: running first client meetings independently within 6 months
- Focus on comprehensive financial planning, not pure sales

### Investment Platform
- Brokerage firm model (not wirehouse like Fidelity/JP Morgan; not insurance captive)
- Access to all investment options: Fidelity/Vanguard ETFs (advisory), Principal (commission)
- Client ownership advantage vs. traditional firms

### Success Factors
- Key trait: grit and persistence through high rejection rates
- Critical milestone: 3-year survival point — compensation significantly improves after this

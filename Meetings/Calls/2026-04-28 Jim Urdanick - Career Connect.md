## Key Advice — Career Direction

### Financial Analyst vs. Accounting
- FA roles are more interesting than accounting grunt work
- FA gives insight into **future growth planning** vs. historical reporting
- Better foundation for entrepreneurial goals long-term
- AI will replace lower-level bookkeeping faster than analytical work → FA safer and more valuable

### Company Size
- Easier to move **large → small** than reverse — start bigger if possible
- Small companies: more C-suite access, more meaningful contribution
- **PE-backed companies in niche markets** (e.g. eDiscovery) = strong growth opportunities
- Private equity advantages: less regulatory headache than public company reporting; analytical work on debt-backed growth companies; better career mobility

### AI & Future-Proofing
- Learn to interpret data **contextually** — AI can't provide full business nuance
- Focus on **planning and forecasting** rather than historical analysis
- Develop people skills alongside technical abilities

### CPA
- "Nice to have" not "need to have" for the analyst track John is on
- Only pursue if company sponsors it or a consulting role requires it
- Won't limit opportunities without it given career goals

### International Experience
- **Highlight prominently on resume** — shows curiosity, worldliness, business perspective beyond domestic market
- This is a genuine differentiator

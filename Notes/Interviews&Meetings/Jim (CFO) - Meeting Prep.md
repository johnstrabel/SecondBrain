# Meeting Prep — Jim (CFO, Heather's Company)

## Who Is Jim

- **Role:** CFO at Heather King's company (Washington, DC area)
- **Background:** ~30-year career in finance and accounting; rose through the ranks to CFO level; has held CFO roles at multiple companies
- **How you got here:** Heather led a 6-month CFO search with her COO and recently hired him — she vouched for him as "a super nice guy"
- **Why he's valuable:** He's been where you want to go. He came up through finance/accounting and reached the top of the function — his path is directly analogous to the FA1 roles you're targeting

---

## Your Goal for This Call

This is an **informational interview**, not a job interview. Use Heather's exact framework:

- **75–80% of the call:** Share your background, what you're targeting, ask for his insights and feedback
- **Final 5 min:** Two specific asks (see below)

---

## How to Open

Keep it warm, concise, and genuine. Something like:

> *"Jim, thank you so much for taking the time — I really appreciate Heather connecting us. She spoke very highly of you and your career. I'm at a launching point right now — finishing my master's in finance in Prague, heading back to the US, and actively looking for financial analyst roles. I'm really flexible and open to where I land, and I'd love to get your perspective on how to think about this search."*

Then **let him talk.** Ask questions and listen. Don't pitch yourself like it's an interview.

## Questions to Ask Jim

### On his career path
- *"You've had a 30-year career rising to CFO — what did those early financial analyst roles look like, and what made the difference between people who moved up and those who stayed flat?"*
- *"Looking back, what's the thing you wish someone had told you in your first 2–3 years in finance?"*
- *"What do you think separates a strong FA1/FA2 from the pack in today's environment?"*

### On the job market / targeting
- *"I'm primarily looking at financial analyst roles — reporting, forecasting, ad hoc analysis, BI. Does that feel like the right entry point given my background, or would you redirect me?"*
- *"Are there industries or company types you'd steer toward or away from for a first role?"*
- *"Any thoughts on large corporate FA programs vs. smaller companies where you'd have more immediate responsibility?"* *(This mirrors Heather's two-path framework — structured training vs. early autonomy)*

### On your specific situation
- *"I'm coming back from two years in Prague — does the international experience read well, or do I need to reframe it for domestic employers?"*
- *"My thesis is pretty technical (ML, NLP, Python) — do you think that differentiates me in traditional FA roles, or is it irrelevant?"*
